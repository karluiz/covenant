Inference as a routing matrix, not a stack of cards

Six roles, each on its own provider + model. Today they're six tall, near-identical cards — you scroll to see two at a time and can't compare the routing at a glance. The tab's whole job is one decision: cheap where it's frequent, powerful where it counts. The redesign makes that decision visible.

source: ui/src/settings/model_routes.ts · 6 roles · unchanged data model

What's off today

  • Provider + model stacked vertically → each route is ~260px tall; only 2 fit on screen.
  • Six identical cards, no visual hierarchy — the hot-path routes look the same as the rare ones.
  • The one insight the intro sells — mix cheap & frequent vs. powerful & rare — is nowhere in the layout.
  • Status + hint are two separate full-width lines of prose per card.

What the redesign does

  • One route = one row. All six visible without scrolling; provider→model inline.
  • Grouped by cadence — the structure teaches the cost tradeoff it's asking you to make.
  • A frequency meter per row: how hot the path is, so "keep it cheap" is obvious.
  • Reachability collapses to one compact chip. Same selects, same backend — pure layout.
